Interactive Crime Data Visualizations

These interactive charts explore trends in reported crimes and arrests across Albany, NY (2021–2024), offering insight into demographics, neighborhood activity, and temporal dynamics.

Choropleth-style summary of arrests by neighborhood.

Reported crimes by day of the week. Fridays and Saturdays consistently show higher volume.

Temporal heatmap showing frequency of arrests by hour and weekday.

Reported crime density by time of day across the week.

Gender distribution among arrestees. Male arrests are the majority across all zones.

Breakdown of top arrest offenses by race (2021–2024).

Geographic concentration of reported crime incidents in Albany city limits.

Figure: Predicted violent crime rates by zone using a 2023 regression model. Model inputs include population growth, minority percentage, migration pressure, and police presence index. High-risk zones include Albany–Arbor Hill and Schenectady–Central.

Source: Albany Police Department FOIL data (2021–2024), processed by the Albany Aegis Initiative. Visuals produced with Python, Seaborn, and Chart.js.
